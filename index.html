Towards the Future of Robotics

About the Lab & Our Activities


Our lab investigates how to build low-power,
real-time robotic systems that thrive in extreme
and unstructured environments.

We develop spiking neural network-based architectures,
processing spike-based information, and benchmark
energy–latency trade-offs on mobile robots.

Our Research
Key Research Areas

  • Energy-Efficient Solutions for Mobile Robotics

    Developing approaches and architectures that reduce power consumption in mobile robotic platforms, enabling longer operational times and improved sustainability.

  • Neuromorphic Computing with Spiking Neural Networks for Marine Robots

    Investigating the application of SNN-based neuromorphic hardware to enhance perception, navigation, and decision-making in marine robotic systems.

  • Neuromorphic Computing with SNNs for Olfaction Sensing Robots

    Exploring the use of SNNs for efficient and robust olfactory sensing, enabling robots to detect and interpret chemical cues in real-time for applications such as environmental monitoring and hazard detection.
