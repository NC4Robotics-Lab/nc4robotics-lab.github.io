Facilities

Neuromorphic Computing Lab

Neuromorphic Computing Lab is dedicated to pushing the frontiers of brain-inspired computing. The lab hosts cutting-edge neuromorphic hardware platforms, spiking neural network development tools, and high-performance embedded systems. It enables interdisciplinary research in low-power AI, adaptive signal processing, robotics, and edge intelligence.

  • Access to advanced neuromorphic processors
  • FPGA-based prototyping and ASIC design tools
  • Dedicated workstations for large-scale neural simulation
  • Custom development boards for sensor integration and real-time processing
  • Collaborative space for algorithm development and testing

Marine Robotics Pool

The Marine Robotics Pool, located at Khalifa University, provides a controlled testing environment for the development and validation of advanced underwater robotics. This facility supports research in autonomous marine navigation, underwater sensing, multi-robot coordination, and robust perception in dynamic aquatic environments.

  • Large test pool with configurable flow and wave conditions
  • Precision underwater motion capture and tracking systems
  • Support for deployment of ROVs and Surface vehicles
  • Facilities for safe integration, calibration, and recovery operations
Resources

INRC Resources

Our lab leverages the resources of the Intel Neuromorphic Research Community (INRC) to advance research in brain-inspired computing and spiking neural networks. The INRC provides access to state-of-the-art neuromorphic hardware, development tools, collaborative research opportunities, and a vibrant community of researchers pushing the boundaries of adaptive and energy-efficient AI.

  • Remote access to Intel Loihi neuromorphic processors
  • Software frameworks and development tools for spiking neural networks
  • Support for benchmarking neuromorphic algorithms on real hardware
